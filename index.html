About the Power Assessment

The power assessment measures explosive strength through 10 consecutive single-leg bounds, alternating legs with each jump. Athletes take a short 5-meter run-up to build momentum, then perform maximum distance bounds while maintaining balance and control. This test evaluates lower body power, reactive strength, and the ability to transfer force efficiently through the kinetic chain.

About the Reactive Ability Test

The reactive ability test uses the same 10-bound format but with a longer 25-meter run-up to assess how effectively athletes can utilize greater momentum and speed. This test measures the ability to reactively convert horizontal velocity into explosive bounding distance, evaluating the neuromuscular system's capacity to rapidly adapt and generate force under higher speed conditions.

Conclusion & Action Plan

Mason's assessment reveals significant mechanical deficiencies across all speed phases that are directly limiting his performance. The video analysis clearly identifies specific issues: excessive ground contact time (180ms), braking patterns, diagonal projection, and postural dysfunction. These technical problems compound as speed increases, creating performance gaps of 24-54% compared to elite footballers.

The identical bound distances (20.8m) for both short and long run-ins indicate poor reactive ability and inefficient force transfer. Combined with the substantial speed deficits, this suggests Mason requires comprehensive technical reconstruction rather than simply more training volume.

Priority Actions: Immediate focus on acceleration mechanics, ground contact time reduction, and postural correction through targeted technical drills. Address braking patterns and arm mechanics before progressing to higher intensity work.
